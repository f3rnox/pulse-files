Privacy Policy for Pulse Files

Effective date: June 16, 2026

Pulse Files is an Android file manager and storage cleaner for browsing, previewing, editing, organizing, sharing, and deleting files on your device.

Summary

Pulse Files is designed to work locally on your device. The app does not include advertising, analytics, crash reporting, account login, or a network permission, and it does not transmit your files or personal information to the app developer.

Information the app accesses

Pulse Files may access the following information on your device when you grant storage access or use app features:

  • Files and folders in shared device storage, including file names, paths, sizes, modified dates, and file contents when needed to preview, edit, copy, move, compress, extract, share, or delete files.
  • Storage information such as total, used, and free space.
  • File metadata and limited file contents during cleanup scans. Duplicate detection reads a small portion of files to compare likely duplicates.
  • App preferences stored locally, such as sorting choices, theme settings, scan settings, last browsed folder, start folder, and ignored large-file paths.

How the app uses information

Pulse Files uses device storage information only to provide app features, including:

  • Browsing and searching local folders.
  • Showing file details and previews.
  • Editing text files when you choose to save changes.
  • Copying, moving, renaming, deleting, compressing, and extracting files.
  • Scanning for junk files, duplicate files, large files, downloads, and APK installers.
  • Remembering app preferences and folder choices.

Data sharing

Pulse Files does not sell, rent, or share your information with the app developer or advertising partners.

When you choose to open or share a file with another app, Pulse Files uses Android system intents and a FileProvider to give the selected receiving app temporary read access to the file or files you selected. The receiving app's handling of that data is governed by that app's privacy policy.

Data storage and retention

Pulse Files stores app preferences locally on your device using Android SharedPreferences. These preferences remain until you change them, clear the app data, or uninstall the app.

Files on your device remain on your device unless you use Pulse Files or another app to modify or delete them.

Backups

Android device backup or device-transfer features may back up app preferences according to your device settings and Android's backup behavior. Pulse Files does not control Google, device manufacturer, or other system backup services.

Permissions

Pulse Files requests storage-related permissions so it can browse, scan, and manage files:

  • Read external storage on supported Android versions.
  • Write external storage on supported Android versions.
  • Manage all files access on Android versions that require it for full file-manager functionality.

You can revoke permissions through Android system settings. Some app features may stop working without storage access.

Security

Pulse Files relies on Android platform controls and local app storage to protect its data. No method of storage or file sharing is completely secure, so only share files with apps and people you trust.

Children's privacy

Pulse Files is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated when Pulse Files changes how it accesses, uses, stores, or shares data. Updates will be stored in this repository.

Contact

For questions about this policy, contact the project maintainers through the Pulse Files repository at https://github.com/f3rnox/pulse-files.
